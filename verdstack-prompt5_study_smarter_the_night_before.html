5 Ways to Study Smarter the Night Before Your Exam · @verdstack

Exam Prep · Copy-Paste Prompts · Claude AI

How to use this pack: Copy any prompt below, open claude.ai, paste the prompt, fill in the brackets with your actual content, and hit enter. Claude handles the rest. No setup needed.

Way 01

Build a Last-Minute Study Triage

Use this when

You have a few hours left and no idea which topics are actually worth your time.

Your prompt

I have [X hours] before my [subject] exam. Topics covered: [list your topics]. Based on typical exam weighting for this subject, rank these from most to least important and tell me exactly how to split my study time.

💡
Pro tip

Paste your syllabus or a list of past exam topics after the prompt — Claude will prioritize even more accurately based on what actually shows up.

Way 02

Generate a Custom Practice Quiz

Use this when

You want to actively test yourself instead of passively re-reading notes that blur together.

Your prompt

Generate 10 practice exam questions on [topic] at [beginner / intermediate / advanced] level. For each question, give 4 multiple-choice options, mark the correct answer, and explain in 1–2 sentences why it's correct.

💡
Pro tip

After you answer, tell Claude which questions you got wrong — then ask it to generate 10 more focused only on those concepts. Zero in on the gaps fast.

Way 03

Turn Confusion Into a Clear Explanation

Use this when

There's a concept that still doesn't click and you're running out of time to figure it out alone.

Your prompt

I still don't understand [concept] from [subject]. Explain it simply as if I'm a first-year student, then show me a complete worked example from scratch, and finish by telling me the single most important thing to remember about it.

💡
Pro tip

Once the base makes sense, follow up with: "Now go deeper — what are the edge cases or common exam traps with this concept?" Two messages, full understanding.

Way 04

Build Memory Aids for Key Formulas

Use this when

You need to memorize equations, definitions, or lists — and generic repetition isn't cutting it.

Your prompt

I need to memorize [formula / concept / list of definitions] for tomorrow's exam. Create three memory aids: one mnemonic acronym, one analogy to something everyday, and one short story that captures the logic. Make them capture the meaning — not just the letters.

💡
Pro tip

Ask for three versions and pick whichever sticks. A story, an acronym, and an analogy — different hooks work for different minds. Let Claude generate the options.

Way 05

Do a Rapid Brain Dump Review

Use this when

You want to find your real blind spots before the exam does — fast, without guessing.

Your prompt

Here's everything I remember about [topic] — it might be incomplete or wrong: [your brain dump]. Please: (1) identify any factual errors, (2) list the key concepts I'm missing, and (3) rank what I most urgently need to review before tomorrow's exam.

💡
Pro tip

Do this without looking at your notes first. Writing from memory — even badly — is the fastest way to surface what you actually don't know before the exam does.
