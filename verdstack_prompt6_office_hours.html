Office Hours· STEM Students· Copy-Paste Prompts· Claude AI

How to use this pack: Open claude.ai — free, no install needed. Work through Steps 01–04 before your next office hours session. Each prompt builds on the last. By the end, you'll have one precise, written question ready to hand your professor.

Step 01

Brain Dump What You Know

Use this when

You're confused about a concept but can't explain what specifically you don't understand. You need to get your messy thinking out first.

Your prompt
Here's everything I think I know about [concept]: [write your brain dump — messy, incomplete, and possibly wrong is fine]. Where does my understanding break down? What is the first thing I'm getting wrong?
💡
Pro tip

Do this without looking at your notes. Your raw, unfiltered understanding is more useful here than a polished summary — the gaps show up more clearly when you haven't cleaned it up.

Step 02

Turn the Gap Into a Precise Question

Use this when

You've found where your understanding breaks down and now need to turn that gap into a question a professor can actually answer in 5 minutes.

Your prompt
My confusion is specifically about [describe the gap Claude identified]. Help me write a precise, one-sentence question I can ask my professor that they can fully answer in under 5 minutes. Give me 3 versions to choose from.
💡
Pro tip

Pick the version that feels most like what you actually mean — then read it out loud. If it sounds like something a confident student would ask, it's ready. If it sounds vague, ask Claude to tighten it further.

Step 03

Anticipate the Follow-Up Questions

Use this when

You have your question ready but want to avoid being caught off guard when your professor asks what you've already tried or what you already understand.

Your prompt
I'm going to ask my professor this question: [paste your chosen question from Step 02]. What are the 3 most likely follow-up questions they will ask me, and what should I know or have ready to answer each one?
💡
Pro tip

Review Claude's follow-up answers briefly before you walk in. You don't need to memorise them — just having read them means you'll answer more naturally instead of going blank under pressure.

Step 04

Write the Final One-Sentence Question

Use this when

You're about to walk into office hours and want the sharpest, clearest version of your question written down and ready to hand over.

Your prompt
Refine this into the clearest, most specific one-sentence question I can write at the top of my notes before walking into office hours: [paste your question]. It should be direct, show that I've thought about it, and be easy for a professor to give a focused answer to.
💡
Pro tip

Write the final question at the top of a blank page and hand it to your professor first. Students who do this get longer, better answers — it signals preparation and makes the professor's job easier.

Bonus

Can't Make Office Hours? Write the Email Instead.

Use this when

You can't attend office hours but still need an answer. A precise email question gets a precise reply — not "come see me in person."

Your prompt
Help me write a short email to my professor asking about [specific question from Step 04]. Keep it under 5 sentences, respectful in tone, and phrased so it's easy to answer in a single reply. Include one sentence about what I've already tried.
💡
Pro tip

The sentence about what you've already tried is the most important part. It tells your professor you're not asking them to do your thinking for you — and that alone doubles the quality of the answer you'll get back.

How to Get the Most Out of Office Hours

01

Do Step 01 without your notes open. Your raw confusion is more useful than a polished summary — the gaps show up when you haven't cleaned the thinking up first.

02

Work through Steps 01–04 in order. Each one builds on the last. Don't skip to Step 04 — the question you write without the earlier steps will still be vague.

03

Open claude.ai, paste each prompt, and fill in the brackets with your actual content. The more specific you are, the more precise Claude's output.

04

Write the final question on paper before you walk in. Not on your phone. On paper. Having it written anchors you when nerves kick in.

05

After office hours, ask Claude to explain whatever you learned in the session. Verbalising it to Claude while it's fresh locks in the understanding before it fades.
