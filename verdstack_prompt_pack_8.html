Analyze Your Graded Assignment and Build a Study Plan · @verdstack

Learn From Mistakes· STEM Students· Copy-Paste Prompts· Claude AI

How to use this pack: Open claude.ai — free, no install needed. Work through Steps 01–04 in order using your most recent graded assignment. Do this within 24 hours of getting it back while the material is still fresh. The Bonus step works best when you have 3 or more assignments from the same course.

Step 01

Feed Claude Your Work and All Feedback

Use this when

You've just gotten a graded assignment back and want to extract every useful signal from the feedback before filing it away and forgetting it.

Your prompt
Here is my graded assignment for [subject]: [paste your original answers in full]. Here is the feedback and marks I lost: [paste professor comments, point deductions, and any written notes]. Read everything carefully — I'll give you instructions next.
💡
Pro tip

If your feedback is handwritten, photograph it and describe what it says in your own words. Even rough paraphrasing works — "lost 3 marks on question 2, professor wrote 'wrong sign in KVL loop'" gives Claude enough to work with precisely.

Step 02

Categorize and Rank Every Mistake

Use this when

Claude has read your assignment and feedback. Now you need to know which mistakes actually matter and which are one-offs you don't need to worry about.

Your prompt
Go through every mistake I made. For each one: (1) categorize it as a conceptual gap, a careless error, or a misunderstood instruction, (2) explain in plain language what I actually got wrong and why, and (3) rank all mistakes by how much they will hurt me if I repeat them on the final exam.
💡
Pro tip

Focus your time on conceptual gaps first — they're the ones Claude ranks highest. Careless errors fix themselves through repetition and practice. Gaps don't close without deliberate study.

Step 03

Build a Day-by-Day Study Plan From the Gaps

Use this when

You have a ranked list of your mistakes and need to turn them into a concrete, realistic study plan — not a vague intention to "review everything."

Your prompt
Based on the mistakes you ranked, build me a day-by-day study plan for the next [X days] before my exam. Be specific — tell me exactly what concept to review each day, why it's the priority for that day, and roughly how long to spend on it. I have [X hours] available per day. Start with the highest-priority gaps.
💡
Pro tip

Give Claude your realistic available hours — not your ideal number. A plan built around 1 honest hour per day will actually get done. A plan built around 4 optimistic hours gets abandoned by day 2 and makes you feel worse than having no plan at all.

Step 04

Drill Practice Problems for Your Exact Weak Spots

Use this when

You've identified and understood your top conceptual gaps and need to practice them specifically — not generic problems from a textbook that may not target what you actually got wrong.

Your prompt
Generate 5 practice problems that specifically target my top 2 conceptual gaps from this assignment. Make them similar in difficulty and style to what my professor asked. Give them to me one at a time, wait for my answer, give me feedback on whether I'm right and what I missed, then move to the next one.
💡
Pro tip

After each problem, ask: "What would I have needed to know to get this right immediately, without hesitation?" That's the exact knowledge to lock in. Write it down. That list becomes your pre-exam review sheet.

Bonus

Find the Pattern Across All Your Assignments

Use this when

You have 3 or more graded assignments from the same course and want to find which mistakes keep coming back — the recurring pattern that's the real threat on your final exam.

Your prompt
I've given you feedback from [X] assignments this semester in [subject]. Review all of it and tell me: (1) which mistakes appear more than once across assignments, (2) what the underlying pattern or gap is behind those recurring mistakes, and (3) what is the single most urgent concept I need to fix before the final exam and why.
💡
Pro tip

Run this bonus step 2 weeks before your final — not the night before. The pattern Claude identifies at that stage is your actual study priority for the entire course. It's more valuable than any generic study guide.

How to Get the Most Out of This Process

01

Do this within 24 hours of getting the assignment back. The material is freshest then, and the feedback still stings enough to motivate you to actually fix it.

02

Paste your full original answers — not just the questions. Claude needs to see what you actually wrote to understand the thinking behind the mistake, not just the fact that you got it wrong.

03

Open claude.ai and do Steps 01 and 02 in the same conversation so Claude has full context before it starts categorizing your mistakes.

04

Do Step 04 yourself — don't just read Claude's explanations. Active practice on your specific gaps is what closes them. Reading about them gives you the feeling of progress without the actual progress.

05

Save the mistake list Claude generates from Step 02 somewhere you'll see it before the exam. It's a better pre-exam checklist than anything you'd make from scratch.
