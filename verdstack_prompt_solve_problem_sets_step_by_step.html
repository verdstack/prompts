5 Ways to Use Claude to Solve Problem Sets Step by Step · @verdstack

Problem Solving· STEM Students· Copy-Paste Prompts· Claude AI

How to use this pack: Open claude.ai — free, no install needed. Start a new conversation, copy any prompt below, paste in your actual problem, and let Claude guide you. The key rule: always try the problem yourself first before asking for the full solution.

Way 01

When You're Stuck — First Step Only

Use this when

You've read the problem but have no idea where to even begin. You need a nudge, not the answer.

Your prompt
Here is the problem: [paste problem]. I am stuck and do not know how to start. Do NOT solve it for me. Just tell me: (1) what concept or method this problem is testing, and (2) what the very first step should be. Then stop and let me try.
💡
Pro tip

After Claude gives you the first step, attempt it yourself — then come back and say "here's what I did next, is this right?" This back-and-forth is how real understanding is built.

Way 02

Full Step-by-Step Walkthrough

Use this when

You've attempted the problem, checked your answer, and still don't understand why the correct solution works the way it does.

Your prompt
Solve this problem step by step: [paste problem]. For each step, explain: (1) what you are doing, (2) why you are doing it — not just the calculation. Use plain language. Flag any step where students commonly make mistakes and explain why those mistakes happen.
💡
Pro tip

After reading the walkthrough, close it and try to reproduce the full solution from memory. If you get stuck, check one step, then continue. Repeat until you can do it without looking at all.

Way 03

Check My Work

Use this when

You've attempted the problem and want to know not just whether you're right, but exactly where and why you went wrong if you aren't.

Your prompt
Here is the problem: [paste problem]. Here is my attempt: [paste your full working and answer]. Check my solution. Do not just tell me if it is right or wrong — find the exact step where I went wrong, explain the conceptual mistake I made, and tell me what I should have done instead at that step.
💡
Pro tip

A wrong attempt you understand is worth more than a correct answer you copied. The mistake Claude identifies is exactly what would have cost you marks in the real exam.

Way 04

Understand the Concept First

Use this when

The problem involves a method or theorem you recognise but don't fully understand. You want the intuition before the mechanics.

Your prompt
Before solving this problem: [paste problem]. First explain: (1) what concept or theorem this is testing, (2) a real-world analogy that makes the concept intuitive, (3) the general approach for this type of problem and why it works. Then solve it step by step using that framework.
💡
Pro tip

Ask Claude to give the analogy "as if I have never taken this course before." The simpler the analogy, the deeper the intuition it builds — and the easier it is to recall under exam pressure.

Way 05

Generate Similar Problems to Drill

Use this when

You've understood one problem and want to lock in the concept by practising variations before it fades from memory.

Your prompt
I just understood how to solve this type of problem: [describe the problem type or paste the original]. Generate 5 new problems that test the same concept — start easy and get progressively harder with each one. Do not solve them yet. Give me one at a time, wait for my answer, give me feedback, then move to the next.
💡
Pro tip

For the last and hardest problem, ask Claude to add one unexpected twist — a boundary condition, a sign change, or a real-world context. Exam questions are always familiar concepts with one detail you didn't drill for.

The Golden Rule for Using These Prompts

01

Always attempt the problem first — even if you're not confident. Write something down before asking Claude anything.

02

Use Way 01 if you're stuck at the start. Use Way 03 if you've attempted it and want feedback. Use Way 02 only after you've genuinely tried.

03

Open claude.ai, paste your prompt, and include the full problem text. The more context you give, the more precise Claude's guidance.

04

After any walkthrough, close it and reproduce the solution from memory. Real understanding means being able to do it without looking.

05

End every problem session with Way 05 — at least 3 variations. One problem understood is not enough. Three problems understood is a pattern locked in.
