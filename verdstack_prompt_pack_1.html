5 Ways to Use Claude to Plan Your Schedule · @verdstack

Schedule Planning · Productivity · Copy-Paste Prompts · Claude AI

How to use this pack: Open claude.ai — it's free. Start a new conversation, copy any prompt below, fill in the brackets with your real information, and hit enter. Use these prompts at the start of each week for best results. The more honest and specific you are about your schedule, the better Claude's plan will be.

Way 01

Weekly Time-Blocking

Use this when

It's Sunday evening and you want to go into Monday with a clear, realistic plan instead of winging it day by day.

Your prompt
Here are my tasks for the week: [list every task, meeting, assignment, and commitment]. My peak focus hours are [morning / afternoon / evening]. Block my calendar Monday to Friday — protect 2 × 90-minute deep-work slots per day, group similar tasks together, add 15-minute transition buffers between meetings, and flag any day that looks overloaded.
💡
Pro tip

Tell Claude which tasks need deep focus vs which are low-effort admin — it will schedule cognitively demanding work during your peak hours and save easy tasks for when your energy dips.

Way 02

Daily Prioritisation

Use this when

You have a long task list and no idea what to tackle first — use this every morning before you open your laptop.

Your prompt
Here are all my tasks for today: [list everything]. I have [N] hours of focused time available and my energy is [high / medium / low] right now. Rank these by urgency and impact, give me a clear top 3 to complete before anything else, schedule the rest realistically around my energy levels, and tell me what I should drop or defer if time runs short.
💡
Pro tip

Be honest about your energy level — if you say "high" when you're exhausted, Claude will schedule hard tasks you won't actually do. Honest input leads to a plan you'll follow.

Way 03

Project Breakdown

Use this when

You have a big deadline coming up and it feels too overwhelming to know where to even start.

Your prompt
I need to [describe the project or deliverable] by [deadline date]. I can work on it approximately [N] hours per week. Break it into weekly milestones and daily sub-tasks, flag any risks or dependencies I should watch out for, and tell me exactly what I should start working on this week — today if possible.
💡
Pro tip

Ask Claude to add a buffer day before the final deadline — a full day with no new tasks, just for reviewing and polishing. It's the single biggest quality improvement you can make to any project.

Way 04

Meeting Prep & Recap

Use this when

You want to walk into every meeting sharp and walk out with clear action items — without spending an hour preparing or writing notes.

Before the meeting
I have a [type of meeting — e.g. study group, project sync, lecture, 1-on-1] with [who] about [topic]. Give me 5 sharp questions I should ask or points I should raise, the key outcome I should walk away with, and anything I should prepare or review beforehand.
After the meeting
Here are my rough notes from the meeting: [paste notes]. Summarise the key decisions made, extract all action items with who owns each one, and draft a short follow-up message I can send to attendees in under 2 minutes.
💡
Pro tip

Even bullet-point, messy notes work fine — Claude doesn't need perfect formatting. Just brain-dump everything you remember and it will structure it for you.

Way 05

Friday Weekly Review

Use this when

It's the end of the week and you want to reflect on what happened, spot patterns, and go into next week with a head start — in under 10 minutes.

Your prompt
This week I completed: [list what got done]. I didn't finish: [list what didn't]. What drained my energy most: [describe]. What felt easiest or most productive: [describe]. Identify any patterns you notice, tell me the one thing I should change or protect next week, and draft a basic Monday morning plan to start the week with momentum.
💡
Pro tip

Do this review every Friday for 4 weeks in a row — then ask Claude to compare all 4 weeks and find your biggest recurring productivity pattern. The compounded insight is incredibly useful.
