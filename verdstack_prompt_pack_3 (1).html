How to use this pack: At the start of each semester, open claude.ai and work through these prompts in order — or jump straight to Way 05 (the master prompt) if you want everything at once. Fill in the brackets with your real courses, dates, and constraints. The more detail you give Claude, the more useful the output.

Way 01

Course Load Audit

Use this when

You're about to start a semester and want to know if your course combination is manageable — before it's too late to drop a class.

Your prompt
Here are my courses this semester: [list each course + credit hours + brief description of what it covers]. Rate each course by difficulty (1–5), identify which weeks will overlap the most, flag any combinations that are high-risk, and tell me if my total workload is manageable or if I should consider dropping one.
💡
Pro tip

Tell Claude which courses you've already struggled with before — it adjusts the difficulty rating to your personal history, not just the course's general reputation.

Way 02

Deadline Timeline

Use this when

You want to see every assignment, lab, project, and exam across all courses laid out in one place — so nothing sneaks up on you.

Your prompt
Here are all my deadlines this semester across all courses: [list each item with its date and course name]. Map them week by week from Week 1 to Week 16. Highlight any week with 3 or more overlapping deadlines as a danger week. Then suggest which tasks I should start early to reduce the crunch, and which weeks I'll have breathing room to get ahead.
💡
Pro tip

Ask Claude to format the output as a table — then paste it directly into Notion or Google Sheets for a live tracker you can check off each week.

Way 03

Weekly Study Budget

Use this when

You want to stop spreading study time equally across all subjects and start investing your hours where they actually matter most.

Your prompt
My courses this semester are: [list with difficulty level if known]. I have [N] free hours per week for studying outside of class time. My other weekly commitments are: [e.g. part-time work 10hrs, gym 3×, club meetings]. Based on each course's difficulty and upcoming deadlines, give me a weekly study budget — how many hours per subject per week — and adjust it dynamically for my 3 hardest weeks in the semester.
💡
Pro tip

Include your non-study commitments honestly. Claude plans around real life, not ideal conditions — and it will protect the things that matter to your wellbeing too.

Way 04

Survival Week Planning

Use this when

You know a brutal week is coming and want a day-by-day battle plan built before it hits — not the night before.

Your prompt
Week [X] is my hardest week this semester. I have: [list every deadline, exam, lab report, and personal commitment that week]. I can study from [start time] to [end time] each day. Build me a day-by-day plan for that entire week — what to focus on each day, in what order, with realistic time blocks, built-in breaks, and a buffer day before the most important deadline.
💡
Pro tip

Run this prompt 2 weeks before the crunch week arrives — not the night before. The earlier you plan, the more Claude can front-load heavy tasks into lighter days before it hits.

Way 05

The Master Semester Prompt

Use this when

It's week 1 and you want a complete semester strategy — all five outputs in a single conversation. This is the one to bookmark.

Your prompt — copy everything below
I'm a [year] [major] student. My courses this semester are: [list each course + credit hours]. All deadlines across all courses: [list with dates and course names]. Study hours available: [N hours/day], [which days]. Weekly commitments outside study: [work, gym, clubs, etc.]. My academic goals this semester: [e.g. GPA target, pass certain courses, apply for internships]. Please build me: (1) a course difficulty ranking with a brief reason for each, (2) a week-by-week deadline map with danger weeks flagged, (3) a weekly study budget per subject, (4) a day-by-day survival plan for my top 3 danger weeks, (5) one specific piece of study advice for each course based on its nature.
💡
Pro tip

Save this Claude conversation and return to it weekly. Tell Claude what changed — a new deadline, a dropped module, a week you fell behind — and it updates the plan without starting over.
