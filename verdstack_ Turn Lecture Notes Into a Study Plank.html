5 Ways to Turn Lecture Notes Into a Study Plan · @verdstack

Study Smarter · Copy-Paste Prompts · Claude AI

How to use this pack: Copy any prompt below, open claude.ai, paste the prompt, fill in the brackets with your actual content, and hit enter. Claude handles the rest. No setup needed.

Way 01

Summarise & Simplify

Use this when

You've just finished a lecture and need to digest it fast before the details fade.

Your prompt

Here are my notes from [topic]: [paste your notes]. Summarise into: (1) the 5 key concepts, (2) one-sentence explanation for each, (3) any formulas or rules I must memorise. Use simple language.

💡
Pro tip

Ask Claude to explain each concept "as if I'm a freshman" — simpler language means better retention and faster recall under exam pressure.

Way 02

Find Your Weak Spots

Use this when

You think you understand the material but want to confirm before the exam reveals otherwise.

Your prompt

Based on these notes: [paste your notes]. Ask me 5 quick questions to test my understanding. Then tell me which concepts I'm weakest on and what I should review first.

💡
Pro tip

Answer honestly — even write "I'm not sure." Claude will pinpoint exactly where to focus your next study block instead of wasting time on things you already know.

Way 03

Build a Spaced Repetition Schedule

Use this when

You have multiple exam topics and need to stop cramming the night before.

Your prompt

My exam topics are: [list topics]. My exam is on [date]. I can study [N] hours/day on [days of the week]. Build a spaced repetition schedule — each topic reviewed at least 3 times, spaced further apart each time.

💡
Pro tip

Tell Claude your weak topics explicitly — it will front-load those with more frequent review sessions and gradually reduce them as exam day approaches.

Way 04

Generate Practice Questions

Use this when

You want to actively test yourself — the most effective study method backed by science.

Your prompt

From these notes: [paste your notes]. Create 10 exam-style questions — mix of MCQ, short answer, and problem-solving. After I answer each one, give me feedback and the correct explanation.

💡
Pro tip

Add "make them harder than you think the exam will be" to the prompt — being overprepared is the best feeling walking into an exam hall.

Way 05

The Master Study Plan Prompt

Use this when

You want a complete, structured day-by-day plan from today all the way to exam day.

Your prompt

Subject: [subject name]. Topics: [list all topics]. Weak areas: [list your weak spots]. Exam date: [date]. Study time available: [N hours/day], [days available]. Build a day-by-day study plan with: daily goals, which topics to cover each day, revision slots for weak areas, and schedule a mock exam session 2 days before the real exam.

💡
Pro tip

Copy the output into Notion, Google Calendar, or even a notes app — then follow it like a contract with yourself. The more specific you are in the prompt, the more realistic the plan Claude builds.
